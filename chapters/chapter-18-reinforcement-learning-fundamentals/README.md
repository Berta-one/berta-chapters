# Chapter 18: Reinforcement Learning Fundamentals

**Track**: Advanced | **Time**: 12 hours | **Prerequisites**: Chapters 1, 3, 6, 9

---

Supervised learning copies a labelled answer; **reinforcement learning** discovers behaviour by trial and error, optimizing long-run reward. This chapter builds the core machinery: **Markov Decision Processes**, **value functions**, the **Bellman** equations, **temporal-difference** learning, and **Q-learning**. You implement a gridworld environment and train a tabular agent to find the optimal path from scratch — then connect it to the policy-gradient and deep-RL ideas behind modern systems.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. **Formalize problems as MDPs** — states, actions, transitions, rewards, discount
2. **Define value functions** V(s) and Q(s, a) and the Bellman equations they satisfy
3. **Explain exploration vs exploitation** and the epsilon-greedy strategy
4. **Implement temporal-difference learning** and contrast it with Monte Carlo
5. **Implement tabular Q-learning** and train an agent to optimality
6. **Tune** discount, learning rate, and exploration schedules
7. **Recover the optimal policy** from a learned Q-table
8. **Connect** tabular methods to DQN and policy-gradient methods (REINFORCE)
