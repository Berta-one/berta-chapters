# Chapter 17: Advanced RAG & Knowledge Systems

**Track**: Advanced | **Time**: 10 hours | **Prerequisites**: Chapters 1, 13

---

Basic RAG retrieves the top-k nearest chunks and hopes for the best. **Advanced RAG** treats retrieval as a system: combine **sparse** (BM25) and **dense** (embedding) signals, **fuse** their rankings, **rerank** the survivors, and **evaluate** the whole pipeline with retrieval metrics. This chapter implements BM25 from scratch, cosine dense retrieval, **Reciprocal Rank Fusion**, a lightweight reranker, and the chunking and metadata strategies that decide whether your knowledge system actually answers questions — all offline with NumPy.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. **Implement BM25** from scratch and explain term frequency, IDF, and length normalization
2. **Build dense retrieval** with cosine similarity over embeddings
3. **Fuse rankings** with Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF) to combine sparse and dense signals
4. **Rerank** a candidate set with a cross-encoder-style scorer
5. **Chunk documents** with size/overlap trade-offs and attach metadata for filtering
6. **Evaluate retrieval** with Recall@k, MRR, and nDCG
7. **Reason about hybrid search** — when sparse beats dense and vice versa
8. **Design a production knowledge system** with caching, indexing, and quality monitoring

## What You'll Build

- **BM25 ranker** — full Okapi BM25 with IDF and length normalization
- **Dense retriever** — cosine similarity ranking over embedding vectors
- **RRF fusion** — combine any number of rankings into one robust order
- **Reranker** — re-score the top candidates with a phrase-aware scorer
- **Evaluation harness** — Recall@k, MRR, and nDCG over a labelled query set
