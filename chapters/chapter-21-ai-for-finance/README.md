# Chapter 21: AI for Finance

**Track**: Advanced | **Time**: 10 hours | **Prerequisites**: Chapters 1, 3, 4, 6, 7

---

Finance is where statistics meets money, and the data fights back. This chapter applies ML to financial problems with the rigor the domain demands: computing **returns** correctly, measuring risk with the **Sharpe ratio** and **maximum drawdown**, building a **moving-average crossover** strategy, and **backtesting** it without fooling yourself. You implement the metrics and a backtester from scratch in NumPy and confront the traps — look-ahead bias, overfitting, and transaction costs — that make naive backtests lie.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. **Compute returns** (simple and log) and explain when to use each
2. **Measure risk-adjusted return** with the annualized Sharpe ratio
3. **Quantify downside** with maximum drawdown
4. **Build a signal** from a moving-average crossover
5. **Backtest** a strategy and produce an equity curve
6. **Avoid look-ahead bias** by lagging signals correctly
7. **Account for costs** and understand why they dominate high-turnover strategies
8. **Reason about overfitting** and the dangers of multiple-testing on price data

## What You'll Build

- **Returns toolkit** — simple and log returns from a price series
- **Risk metrics** — annualized Sharpe ratio and maximum drawdown
- **MA crossover signal** — fast/slow moving averages into long/flat positions
- **Backtester** — lagged signals into an equity curve, costs included
- **Bias checks** — a look-ahead detector and a turnover/cost estimator


