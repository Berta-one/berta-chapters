# Chapter 19: Model Optimization & Inference

**Track**: Advanced | **Time**: 8 hours | **Prerequisites**: Chapters 1, 9, 15

---

A model that is accurate but too slow or too large never ships. **Model optimization** shrinks and speeds up models with minimal accuracy loss: **quantization** (float32 to int8), **pruning** (removing weights), and **knowledge distillation** (training a small student from a large teacher). This chapter implements symmetric int8 quantization, magnitude pruning, and temperature-scaled distillation from scratch in NumPy, and frames the latency/throughput/memory trade-offs that govern real inference.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. **Quantize weights** to int8 with a scale factor and bound the reconstruction error
2. **Prune** weights by magnitude and measure the accuracy/sparsity trade-off
3. **Distill** a small student from a large teacher with temperature-scaled soft targets
4. **Reason about precision** — float32 vs float16 vs int8 and where each is safe
5. **Estimate model size** and memory footprint before and after optimization
6. **Trade off latency vs throughput** with batching
7. **Profile** an inference path and find the bottleneck
8. **Choose an optimization** appropriate to the deployment target (edge, server, batch)

## What You'll Build

- **int8 quantizer** — symmetric quantize/dequantize with measured error bounds
- **Magnitude pruner** — zero the smallest weights to a target sparsity
- **Distillation loss** — temperature-scaled softmax soft targets
- **Size estimator** — bytes-per-parameter accounting across precisions
- **Batching model** — latency vs throughput curve from a simple cost model
