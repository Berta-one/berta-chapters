# Chapter 22: AI Safety & Alignment

**Track**: Advanced | **Time**: 8 hours | **Prerequisites**: Chapters 1, 9, 11

---

As models grow more capable and more widely deployed, **safety** and **alignment** become engineering requirements, not afterthoughts. This chapter makes them concrete: you build a **content filter**, score a **reward model** on human preferences (the heart of RLHF), measure **fairness** with demographic parity and disparate impact, and run a **red-team** harness that probes a model for unsafe behaviour. The emphasis is on *measurable* safety — turning principles into numbers you can track.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. **Map the risk landscape** — misuse, accidents, bias, and emergent failures
2. **Build a content filter** and reason about precision/recall trade-offs in safety
3. **Score a reward model** on preference pairs (the signal behind RLHF)
4. **Measure fairness** with demographic parity difference and disparate impact ratio
5. **Red-team** a model with adversarial prompts and a judge
6. **Understand alignment** approaches: RLHF, constitutional methods, and their limits
7. **Reason about interpretability** and why transparency aids safety
8. **Connect** safety practice to governance and regulation (Chapter 25)

## What You'll Build

- **Content filter** — keyword/pattern blocking with match reporting
- **Reward-model scorer** — preference accuracy on chosen-vs-rejected pairs
- **Fairness metrics** — demographic parity difference and disparate impact (4/5ths rule)
- **Red-team harness** — run adversarial prompts through a model and tally failures
- **Bias audit** — group-wise positive rates and the worst-case disparity

## Quick Start


2. **Create a virtual environment and install dependencies**
   ```bash
   python -m venv .venv

   ```

